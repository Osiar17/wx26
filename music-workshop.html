Wade in

Beneath the Ministry

True ministry begins with the Christian before God.

Wade-In is The Flow Experience’s dedicated space for deeper formation—a season set apart to step beyond the familiar rhythms of weekly ministry and attend to the life, the craft and the purpose beneath them.

Wade-In begins with our first duty: to be Christians who live like Christ. It brings us back to why and how we serve—not merely to improve what we do, but to make sure it pleases God. Each year, it focuses us on the first principles of formation most needed for the season: our life before God, our stewardship of the gift and our service to His Church.

The Depths of Formation

Three dimensions of our formation

— the Christian, the Musician, and the Performer.

If our service is to please God, then we must be formed rightly. Wade-In addresses three essential areas: who we are, what we’ve developed and how we serve.

The 2026 Current

Stewardship

Every year, Wade-In takes us deeper. The season tells us where.

CCC Apenkwa’s theme for 2026 is Stewardship — the call to be faithful with what God has entrusted. For The Flow Experience, this year’s formation brings faithfulness into focus in two places.

For us, stewardship must be seen in two places: the will and the gift.

Two Currents

Faithfulness runs in two currents — one inward, one outward.

The Inward Current · The Will

Surrender

Being faithful with the will.

“The will is where a person decides whether to hold on or yield.”

The will is one of the deepest things God has given to a person — the seat of choice, desire, obedience, resistance, ambition, preference, and surrender. It is the place where we decide whether to insist on our own way or submit to the Father’s will.

To be faithful with the will is to yield it back to God, so that what we want is submitted to what He wants.

Yield Choice Desire Obedience Resistance Ambition Preference Surrender

The Outward Current · The Gift

Intentionality

Being faithful with the gift.

“The gift is where what God has placed within us becomes visible.”

The gift includes our voices, instruments, musical skill, discipline, sound, understanding, opportunities, and influence. To be faithful with it is to handle it intentionally — not casually, not selfishly, and not merely instinctively.

  • What is this sound meant to serve?
  • What does the room need?
  • How can the gift point beyond the musician to the God who gave it?
Serve Voice Instrument Skill Discipline Sound Understanding Opportunity Influence

For The Flow Experience, our 2026 focus on stewardship gives Wade-In its sub-theme — faithfulness with our will, and with our gift.
